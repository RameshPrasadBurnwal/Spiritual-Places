Vaishno Maa

Vaishno Devi shrine is located in the lap of Trikuta hills which is 13 Kms from base camp Katra,
which is one of the prominent town of District Reasi and 63 kms from Jammu city.
The Shrine is famous pilgrimage centre in Jammu and Kashmir state.
It is managed by Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board since 1986.

  1. The Vaishno Devi Temple is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India,
    located in the Trikuta Mountains in Jammu and Kashmir.
    Here are ten key points about the temple:
  2. Location: The temple is situated at an altitude of about 5,200 feet on the Trikuta Mountains in the state of Jammu and Kashmir,
    near the town of Katra.
  3. Holy Shrine: The main temple enshrines the holy cave where Mata Vaishno Devi is believed to have meditated.
    The shrine does not have any idol,
    but there are three naturally formed rock formations known as the Pindis, representing the three goddesses:
    Maha Kali, Maha Lakshmi, and Maha Saraswati.
  4. Yatra (Pilgrimage): Devotees must undertake a 13-kilometer trek from Katra to reach the temple.
    For those unable to walk, ponies, palanquins,
    and helicopter services are also available.
  5. Spiritual Significance: It is believed that Mata Vaishno Devi grants the wishes of her devotees who come with a pure heart.
    The temple attracts millions of pilgrims every year.
  6. Mata’s Call (Bulawa): Pilgrims often say that one cannot visit the temple until Mata Vaishno Devi herself calls them.
    This Bulawa is considered sacred and indicates divine intervention.
  7. 12 km Trek Path: The main trek route is around 12 kilometers long from the base camp in Katra.
    There are shelters, refreshment stalls, and rest stops along the way.
  8. Cave and Water Sources: The cave has three natural water sources, or springs, called Charan Ganga.
    The water is considered holy and is used by pilgrims for purification before entering the cave.
  9. Darshan Timing: The Vaishno Devi shrine is open for pilgrims 24 hours a day throughout the year,
    regardless of the weather conditions.
  10. Bhawan: The main temple complex is called Bhawan,
    where the pilgrims have to register before entering the holy cave.
  11. Festivals and Celebrations: The most popular times to visit the temple are during the Navaratri festival,
    especially during Chaitra and Sharad Navaratri,
    when the shrine witnesses a massive influx of pilgrims.

Shree Ram Prabhu Ji

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ram.
It stands on the site believed to be his birthplace. In 1528, the Babri Masjid was built there by Mughal emperor Babur.
This led to a centuries-long dispute between Hindus and Muslims. After legal battles and a Supreme Court ruling in 2019, the land was awarded to Hindus for temple construction. The foundation stone for the new Ram Mandir was laid on August 5, 2020,
and the temple is expected to be completed by 2024.

  1. Location: The Ram Mandir is being constructed in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh,
    believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram, an important figure in Hinduism.
  2. Religious Significance: The temple is dedicated to Lord Ram, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu,
    and holds immense religious and cultural importance for Hindus worldwide.
  3. Babri Masjid Dispute: In 1528, the Babri Masjid was built on the disputed site,
    believed by Hindus to be the birthplace of Lord Ram, sparking a long-standing conflict over the land.
  4. Demolition of Babri Masjid: On December 6, 1992, the Babri Masjid was demolished by a large crowd of activists, leading to nationwide riots and violence.
  5. Legal Battle: The dispute over the land was fought in Indian courts for decades. In 2010,
    the Allahabad High Court ordered a three-way division of the land,
    but the decision was challenged in the Supreme Court.
  6. Supreme Court Verdict: On November 9, 2019,
    the Supreme Court of India ruled in favor of Hindus,
    granting the entire disputed land for the construction of the Ram Mandir,
    while providing a separate plot of land for the construction of a mosque
  7. Bhoomi Pujan: The foundation stone-laying ceremony (Bhoomi Pujan) for the new temple was held on August 5, 2020, with Prime Minister Narendra Modi attending the historic event.
  8. Architectural Design: The temple is designed in the Nagara style of architecture, featuring intricate carvings, and will stand 161 feet tall with five domes, reflecting ancient Indian temple-building traditions.
  9. Construction Timeline: The temple’s construction is ongoing and is expected to be completed by 2024,
    with the main shrine ready for devotees by early 2024.
  10. Cultural Impact: The Ram Mandir is seen as a symbol of cultural and religious identity for many Hindus,
    representing the culmination of a long struggle for the recognition of the site as Ram's birthplace.
